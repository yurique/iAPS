IMAGE TYPE DETERMINATION (REQUIRED)

After inspecting the image, you MUST first classify the image into exactly one of the following types:
	•	food_photo – a photo of prepared food intended for consumption (e.g., plated meal, bowl, snack, packaged food with visible contents).
	•	menu_photo – a photo of a restaurant menu, menu board, or menu page showing dish names and descriptions.
	•	recipe_photo – a photo of a printed or handwritten recipe, cookbook page, or ingredient list with preparation instructions.

This classification determines which rules apply below.

If the image is ambiguous, choose the most conservative applicable type and explicitly state the uncertainty.

FOR FOOD PHOTOS (food_photo)

Apply all rules in the FOR FOOD PHOTOS (IMAGE ANALYSIS) section.

This includes:
	•	portion estimation from visual evidence
	•	portion vs serving distinction
	•	visual reasoning and counting
	•	authority-based nutrition calculation

FOR MENU PHOTOS (menu_photo)

Menu photos are text-only sources, even though they are images.

You MUST:
	1.	Do not estimate visual portions
	•	Do not infer plate size, portion size, or food quantity.
	2.	Explicitly mark portion as unknown
	•	Set:
	•	portion_estimate = "CANNOT DETERMINE PORTION – menu photo"
	•	assessment_notes = "ESTIMATE ONLY – based on standard reference amounts"
	3.	Use standard reference amounts
	•	Base nutrition on NUTRITION_AUTHORITY standard serving or per-100 g values.
	•	Never multiply by assumed restaurant portions.
	4.	No visual claims
	•	Do not claim to see:
	•	cooking methods
	•	textures
	•	colors
	•	browning or doneness
	•	Treat menu descriptions as text only.
	5.	Absorption context (bounded)
	•	Provide qualitative absorption classification (fast/medium/slow) based on typical preparation.
	•	Explicitly state uncertainty when preparation details are missing.

FOR RECIPE PHOTOS (recipe_photo)

Recipe photos are instructional text, not evidence of prepared food.

You MUST:
	1.	Do not infer cooked appearance
	•	Do not claim the dish is cooked, plated, or served.
	2.	Explicitly mark portion as unknown
	•	Set:
	•	portion_estimate = "CANNOT DETERMINE PORTION – recipe photo"
	•	assessment_notes = "ESTIMATE ONLY – based on standard reference amounts"
	3.	Use standard reference amounts
	•	Base nutrition on NUTRITION_AUTHORITY standard serving or per-100 g values.
	•	Do not assume yield size unless explicitly stated.
	4.	Ingredient-based analysis only
	•	Use listed ingredients and quantities if present.
	•	If quantities are missing or unclear, state the limitation.
	5.	Absorption context (bounded)
	•	Provide qualitative absorption classification (fast/medium/slow) based on ingredients and processing level.
	•	Acknowledge uncertainty when quantities or preparation steps are incomplete.

⚠️ IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTE

If the image is a menu or recipe photo, you MUST NOT:
	•	estimate visual portions
	•	infer serving size
	•	describe food appearance
	•	treat the dish as already prepared or consumed
